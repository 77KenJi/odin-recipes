Baklava

(Image source: Wikimedia Commons)

Description

Baklava is a delicious Middle Eastern dessert made of layers of thin phyllo dough, a spiced mixture of finely chopped nuts, and a sweet syrup poured over it. It's known for its flaky texture and rich, honeyed sweetness, making it a perfect treat for special occasions or anytime you want something indulgent.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of phyllo dough (16 oz), thawed
  • 2 cups of mixed nuts (walnuts, pistachios, or almonds), finely chopped
  • 1 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

For the Syrup:

  • 1 ½ cups of granulated sugar
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Brush the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish with melted butter.
  3. Place one sheet of phyllo dough in the dish and brush with more melted butter. Repeat this process, layering and buttering each sheet (about 8-10 sheets in total).
  4. Mix chopped nuts and ground cinnamon, then spread a thin layer of the nut mixture over the dough.
  5. Continue layering phyllo dough (buttering each sheet) until about half of the dough is used. Spread another layer of nuts. Finish layering the remaining phyllo dough.
  6. Cut the baklava into squares or diamond shapes with a sharp knife before baking.
  7. Bake for 45-50 minutes or until the top is golden brown.
  8. Prepare the syrup by boiling sugar, water, lemon juice, and cinnamon stick. Let it simmer for 10 minutes, then remove the cinnamon stick.
  9. Once the baklava is out of the oven, pour the hot syrup over it and let it cool for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  10. Cut and serve once cooled.
